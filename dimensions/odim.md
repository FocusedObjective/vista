# Outcomes - Decisions - Insights - Measures

ODIM is a framework developed by Larry Maccherone to ensure measures have a clear connection to outcomes and to help people understand their role in decision-making through insights derived from those measures. It's a way to avoid vanity metrics.

ODIM highlights the back-and-forth between measures, insights, decisions, and outcomes, so you can guide people away from vanity metrics and toward real business impact. 
By using both the forward (ODIM) and reverse (MIDO) thinking, you empower teams to anchor every metric they track to a decision and outcome.

Here are some examples for our 5 key metric dimensions -

1. Value:
   Outcome: Increase customer lifetime value (LTV).
   Decision: Which features drive upsell or retention?
   Insight: Which features correlate with higher spend over time?
   Measure: Customer LTV by feature adoption.

2. Impact:
   Outcome: Expand market share in a new segment.
   Decision: Which segment-specific capabilities to invest in?
   Insight: Which capabilities are driving traction in that segment?
   Measure: Adoption rates of segment-specific features.

3. Safety: (We covered this, but for completion)
   Outcome: Fewer customer-facing regressions.
   Decision: When to increase review depth.
   Insight: Which types of changes have caused regressions before.
   Measure: Regression rate by change category.

4. Timing:
   Outcome: Faster delivery of high-value features.
   Decision: Which features to prioritize based on urgency.
   Insight: Which items have the highest cost of delay.
   Measure: Cost of delay per feature.

5. Adaptation:
   Outcome: Fewer repeated mistakes over time.
   Decision: Which review processes to adjust.
   Insight: Which patterns lead to repeated issues.
   Measure: Frequency of recurring issue types post-merge.

Each example ties measures back to decisions, ultimately leading to the outcome you truly care about!
