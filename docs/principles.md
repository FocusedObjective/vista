# Principles

VISTA is built on a set of principles that shape how engineering performance should be understood in the age of AI.

Each principle below has its own page in `docs/principles/`.

## The principles

1. [Output is not outcome](principles/01-output-is-not-outcome.md)
2. [Speed without direction is waste](principles/02-speed-without-direction-is-waste.md)
3. [Work should be judged comparatively, not in isolation](principles/03-judge-work-comparatively.md)
4. [Cost of delay matters](principles/04-cost-of-delay-matters.md)
5. [Risk gates value](principles/05-risk-gates-value.md)
6. [Delivery cost is broader than coding cost](principles/06-delivery-cost-is-broader-than-coding-cost.md)
7. [Review is a learning system](principles/07-review-is-a-learning-system.md)
8. [AI productivity must be measured at the system level](principles/08-ai-productivity-must-be-measured-at-the-system-level.md)
9. [Strategy must be concrete enough to order choices](principles/09-strategy-must-be-concrete-enough-to-order-choices.md)
10. [A healthy system learns](principles/10-a-healthy-system-learns.md)

## Why principles matter

VISTA is not just a list of metrics.

The dimensions of VISTA tell you **what to look at**.

The principles tell you **how to think**.
