# Decision Model

## Why VISTA needs a decision model

VISTA is not just a reporting framework.

It is a framework for making better choices about engineering work in the age of AI.

## The central problem

The central problem is not simply:

**Is this work item good?**

The more important question is:

**Is this the right work to do now, compared with the alternatives, given the value, strategy, risk, timing, and activation realities we face?**

## The VISTA decision rule

**The right next item is the one with the best timing-adjusted strategic upside, subject to acceptable downside risk, sufficient clarity, and realistic verification and activation readiness.**

## The three layers of the model

### 1. Should we do this?
Pre-start decision making.

### 2. How should we do this?
Execution, review, and verification shaping.

### 3. Was it worth it?
Post-merge and post-release evaluation.

## Layer 1: Should we do this?

Main factors:
- expected user or business value
- strategic relevance
- cost of delay
- downside risk
- clarity and readiness
- verification readiness
- activation burden
- AI suitability

### Weights vs gates

**Weights** express preference.  
**Gates** express permissibility.

A useful shorthand is:

**Weights decide preference. Gates decide permissibility.**

## Layer 2: How should we do this?

Main factors:
- review depth
- verification strength
- rollback sensitivity
- privacy and trust sensitivity
- coordination burden
- change complexity
- known failure pattern similarity
- reversibility
- AI involvement and oversight needs

## Layer 3: Was it worth it?

Main factors:
- regressions and hidden post-merge cost
- adoption and repeat usage
- strategic movement
- trust and satisfaction impact
- activation success
- hindsight quality of the priority choice
- reusable learning created

## How VISTA handles uncertainty

VISTA should not pretend to eliminate judgment.

It exists because judgment is required.

That means:
- thresholds vary by context
- startups and legacy enterprises may set different tolerances
- different product domains have different privacy or trust sensitivities

## Summary

The goal is not perfect certainty.

The goal is better judgment under constraint.
