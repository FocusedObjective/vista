# Cost of Delay Matters

## Principle

**Cost of delay matters.**

The best next item is often the one whose delay causes the greatest strategic harm, not simply the one with the highest abstract value.

## Why this matters

Value alone is not enough to prioritize well. Two items can both be valuable, but one may matter much more now because delay would weaken market position, postpone critical learning, or slow strategic capability-building.

## Summary

VISTA uses cost of delay to make prioritization more strategic, comparative, and grounded in the consequences of waiting.
