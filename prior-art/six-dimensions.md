## What the Six Dimensions framework is

The Six Dimensions of Performance extended Larry Maccherone's SDPI by adding two dimensions to create a balanced view of **team delivery performance.** The framework measures how well a team is doing work across six competing dimensions:

1. **Do Lots** — How much work product is flowing? Throughput, velocity.
2. **Do it Fast** — How quickly are items completed? Cycle time, lead time.
3. **Do it Predictably** — How consistent is the pace of delivery? Variability of throughput.
4. **Do it Well** — How good is the quality? Escaped defects, rollbacks, unplanned downtime.
5. **Do Valuable Stuff** — How much value do customers derive from what was delivered? Cost of delay, alignment to strategy, customer satisfaction.
6. **Keep Doing It** — How sustainable is the current performance? Team health, aggregate stability of the other five dimensions.

The first four dimensions came directly from SDPI (Productivity, Responsiveness, Predictability, Quality). The fifth — value — was added to guard against delivering lots of working but useless output. The sixth — sustainability — was renamed from Maccherone's proposed "Happiness" metric to reflect that it serves as an early warning of future performance decline.

The core insight: **improving any single metric obscures the price paid elsewhere.** A team that doubles throughput while quality collapses has not improved. A team with perfect quality but no flow has stalled. The six dimensions force visibility into the trade-offs that individual metrics hide.

## What this framework is not

The Six Dimensions framework is about **team performance measurement**, not work prioritization. It does not tell an organization which items to build, how to rank a backlog, or how to make strategic trade-offs between competing initiatives. It tells a team whether its delivery system is healthy and balanced.

## Why it matters to VISTA

In AI-assisted and agentic engineering, individual team performance — the domain of the Six Dimensions — matters less than it did when teams were the primary constraint.

When AI agents generate code, open pull requests, and resolve issues, the traditional team-level performance metrics (throughput, cycle time, velocity) can spike without any corresponding improvement in outcomes. The delivery system looks healthy by every team-level measure while producing waste at scale.

VISTA builds on the lesson that multi-dimensional measurement is essential, but shifts the focus from team delivery performance to **organizational outcome performance** — whether the right work was chosen, whether it created value, whether it avoided hidden downside, and whether the system learned from what happened.
