# Prior Art

VISTA did not emerge from nothing. It builds on specific earlier work in engineering measurement and performance modeling.

This section documents the frameworks and ideas that shaped VISTA's design, and where they connect to its current structure.

---

## Influences

| Source | Author | Connection to VISTA |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| [Software Development Performance Index (SDPI)](sdpi.md) | Larry Maccherone | Multi-dimensional performance measurement; ODIM framework |
| [Six Dimensions of Performance](six-dimensions.md) | Troy Magennis | Multi-dimensional prioritization; timing, risk, and cost-of-delay modeling |

---

## Why prior art matters

VISTA's position — that engineering performance requires multiple dimensions, not a single metric — is not new. Earlier frameworks demonstrated the same principle with different scope and emphasis.

What VISTA adds is a specific focus on the AI-accelerated engineering environment, where output is cheap and judgment is the constraint. But the measurement philosophy, the insistence on connecting metrics to outcomes, and the multi-dimensional framing all have roots in this prior work.
