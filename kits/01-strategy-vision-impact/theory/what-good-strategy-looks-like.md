# What good strategy looks like for VISTA evaluation

Many teams say they have strategy when they really have:
- aspirations
- themes
- slogans
- broad goals
- a list of good ideas

Those may be useful, but they are often too vague to help with engineering prioritization.

For VISTA, strategy should be clear enough to answer:

- What are we trying to change?
- For whom?
- Why does it matter?
- What capabilities matter most?
- What tradeoffs are we willing to make?
- What should we explicitly not prioritize right now?

## A usable strategy usually contains

### 1. Target
Who are we trying to win with?
Which customers, users, segment, workflow, or market matters most right now?

### 2. Problem
What important problem are we solving for them?
Why is it worth solving?

### 3. Advantage hypothesis
Why do we believe we can win here?
What differentiator, capability, distribution, speed, trust, or cost position matters?

### 4. Time horizon
What matters now versus later?
What is strategically urgent?

### 5. Tradeoffs
What are we willing to defer, reject, or not optimize?
A strategy without tradeoffs is not yet useful enough.

### 6. Evidence
What evidence supports this direction?
Customer demand, usage patterns, revenue signals, operational pain, market pull, or table-stakes requirements all count.

### 7. Measures
How will we know whether the strategy is working?
Not every measure needs to be final, but there should be candidate outcome signals.

## Bad strategy for evaluation sounds like

- "Improve the platform"
- "Delight users"
- "Grow enterprise"
- "Use AI more"
- "Ship faster"

These are directionally positive, but not specific enough to support comparative choice.

## Better strategy for evaluation sounds like

- "Win more mid-market B2B accounts by reducing onboarding friction in the first 14 days."
- "Increase trust and adoption of AI-assisted code generation by reducing review anxiety and production regressions."
- "Become enterprise-ready by closing capability gaps in SSO, auditability, and permissions before adding more workflow features."

## The test

A strategy is usable for VISTA scoring if it helps answer:

- Why should item A come before item B?
- What kind of work should rise in priority?
- What kind of work should drop?
- What evidence would make us change our mind?
