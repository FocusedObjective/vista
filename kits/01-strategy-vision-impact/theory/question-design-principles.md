# Question design principles for VISTA prompts and surveys

Good elicitation is not just about asking more questions.
It is about asking questions in an order that improves clarity.

## Principles

### 1. Start with outcomes before solutions
Ask what should change before asking what should be built.

### 2. Ask open questions before constrained questions
Let people explain in their own words before forcing categories.

### 3. Separate local benefit from strategic consequence
Do not collapse Value and Impact into one question.

### 4. Force comparison
Strategy becomes clearer when people must choose between alternatives.

### 5. Ask for evidence, not just confidence
"What makes you believe this?" is often more useful than "How important is this?"

### 6. Ask about delay
Timing and priority improve when teams articulate the cost of waiting.

### 7. Ask about exclusion
A strategy is clearer when teams state what they are not prioritizing.

### 8. Ask what would change the answer
This surfaces assumptions, risks, and missing evidence.

## Recommended sequence

1. Desired outcome
2. Target user or segment
3. Important problem
4. Strategic relevance
5. Comparative alternatives
6. Evidence
7. Cost of delay
8. Risks and dependencies
9. Success signals
10. Open uncertainties
