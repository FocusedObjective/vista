# VISTA Starter Kit: Strategy, Vision, and Impact

This starter kit helps teams express strategy clearly enough to make better engineering decisions.

It exists because many organizations want to evaluate work against strategy, but do not yet have strategy in a form that is specific enough to support prioritization, scoring, or review depth decisions.

In VISTA terms:

- **Value** asks whether a work item creates meaningful user or business benefit.
- **Impact** asks whether the organization's choices materially advance strategic goals or competitive position.

If strategy is vague, Impact becomes opinion.
If intended benefit is vague, Value becomes theater.

This kit helps teams:
- elicit strategy in a usable form
- connect strategy to engineering evaluation
- challenge backlog items and PRs with better questions
- generate reusable prompts, skills, and surveys
- invite community refinement of the questions themselves

## What this kit contains

- short theory on what makes strategy usable for evaluation
- question banks for strategy and item intake
- prompt templates for LLM use
- skill-style files for reusable challenge flows
- AskPilot survey drafts
- templates for storing outputs in docs, issues, or tooling

## Recommended use order

1. Read `theory/what-good-strategy-looks-like.md`
2. Run the strategy elicitation prompt or survey
3. Produce a short strategy brief
4. Run the item impact challenge against backlog items or PRs
5. Save outputs in the item template
6. Refine the prompts with team and community feedback

## Design principle

This kit is not trying to produce perfect strategy.
It is trying to produce strategy that is clear enough to improve comparative choice.
