# Strategy elicitation question bank

Use these questions to turn vague strategy into something more usable for VISTA evaluation.

## Vision and direction

- What meaningful change are we trying to create over the next 6 to 12 months?
- Which users, customers, or segments matter most right now?
- What problem is most important for them?
- Why is this the right problem to focus on now?

## Strategic choice

- What are we trying to win at?
- What are we deliberately not optimizing right now?
- What capability gaps matter most?
- If we could only materially improve one thing this quarter, what should it be?

## Comparative tradeoffs

- Which kinds of work should rise in priority under this strategy?
- Which kinds of work should fall?
- What would we defer even if it looks locally useful?
- What alternative strategy are we implicitly rejecting?

## Evidence

- What evidence suggests this direction matters?
- What customer, product, sales, support, or operational signals support it?
- Where are we mostly relying on intuition?

## Timing

- Why now?
- What harm occurs if we wait?
- Is there a market, customer, operational, or internal timing reason this matters now?

## Success and learning

- What would early evidence of strategic progress look like?
- What signals would suggest this strategy is not working?
- What decision should these signals help us make better?
